Abstract

We introduce Turning Sand Data into Gold, a framework for transforming weakly labeled or unrefined data into valuable, high-quality training datasets. By integrating iterative representation refinement, selective sampling, and model-guided feedback, our method enhances the effective utility of data and minimizes human labeling overhead. Empirical results across multiple domains demonstrate that the approach consistently improves data efficiency and downstream performance, effectively turning raw “sand” data into predictive “gold”.

Overview

Overview of the Sand2Gold pipeline: noisy raw data is iteratively refined into a curated dataset through embedding-based filtering and self-training cycles.

Stage 1: Data Filtering

Initial pruning removes low-quality or inconsistent samples based on feature similarity and model confidence.

Stage 2: Representation Refinement

Self-supervised embeddings guide progressive re-labeling and weighting, amplifying signal-to-noise ratio.

Stage 3: Iterative Re-mining

The refined model re-mines unlabeled data, closing the loop to produce “gold” data for the final model training.
