Abstract

We present Turning Sand Data into Gold, a framework for transforming weakly supervised or noisy datasets into high-value training data. Our approach introduces a multi-phase refinement pipeline leveraging self-supervised pretraining and sample selection to maximize downstream model performance while minimizing labeling effort.

Method Overview

The Sand2Gold pipeline iteratively filters, enriches, and reweights training data using a synergy between data embeddings and predictive uncertainty estimation. As illustrated below, the model progressively improves its internal representation by “distilling” structure from noise, yielding golden data from raw sand.

Results

Our experiments across diverse benchmarks demonstrate consistent gains in label efficiency and robustness. The framework achieves up to +12% relative improvement on limited data regimes and maintains generalization under domain shifts.
